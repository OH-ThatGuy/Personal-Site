AI Video Editing

Introduction

AI tools have become more prominent than ever before. Tasks that previously took a long time can be completed in mere minutes. One particular field that has been heavily effected by AI is that of video editing. Traditionally, editors can spend hours trimming clips, stiching videos together, and adding effects. There is a balance that is needed between technical skill and time management, but AI is shifting that balance. AI video editing is rapidly changing the speed and accessibility of post-production work, but it is simultaneously raising many questions about authorship and authenticity.

What is AI Video Editing?

"AI video editing" is a very broad term. In truth AI can be used at nearly any stage of post-production with varying levels of human input. I believe that AI video editing can be broken down into two categories: AI assisted editing and AI generated content. AI assisted editing is often used as an additional tool that can help expedite the editing process through automation or the removal of otherwise tedious activities. On the other hand you have AI generated content. This is less of a tool and more of a replacement. Rather than manually edit parts of a video oneself, a user could us Natural Language Processing [NLP] to type a prompt and generate an edited video for them.

For the most part I will be referring to AI assisted video editing throughout this essay, but knowing what AI generated content is helps to build an understanding of why AI assistance is so helpful and why some frown upon AI generated content.

The Pros

If AI is good at one thing, it's automation. AI can take some normally tedious or uninteresting aspects of video editing and have them completed quickly. Some examples of this might be audio balancing or captioning.

AI can also help to lower the barrier of entry into video editing. Most editing interfaces are complicated and take a while to fully grasp. Using NLP, an AI could help someone new to editing figure out how to perform complex cuts or use effects they would otherwise have ignored.

AI also helps to speed up the editing process overall. It can allow the editor to spend their time on more complex effects instead of wasting their time with more menial tasks.

The Cons

The biggest issue that many have with AI is the over-reliance of it. It's easy to get comfortable having an AI perform small tasks for you, but that could progress to AI doing more complex actions until the human input is all but nonexistent. This is where AI assisted editing can become AI generated content.

There is also a fear of homogenization. If every editor made use of the same AI tools to create their content, then all the content would begin to look the same. There is a sort of art or style that each editor has, and AI could take that away if used improperly.

With AI tools making editing so accessible to everyone, where does that leave entry level editing positions? The demand for entry level editors could rapidly diminish as companies become persuaded to use AI rather than a human component, this issue could potentially even trickle down to higher level positions if the technology advances enough.

The Future

The video editing workflow as it is known today is likely to experience a shift in the future, not dissimilar to the switch from traditonal film editing to computer based editing software. AI tools could become a key component in many interfaces, acting as a sort of "co-pilot."

Personalized on-demand video generation is also likely to see a spike in popularity. This would likely coincide with new industry standards and legislations to regulate the sea of new content being produced.

Conclusion

AI video editing has a lot to offer the industry, but it could just as easily end up demolishing it. A balance needs to be struck between efficiency and creativity, with the editor at its center. The future of video editing lies not in replacing editors with AI, but instead in implementing those AI tools into the editing workflow.
